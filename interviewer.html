VR Interveiw Simulator

InterVeiwR was created in Unity using C#. It was a senior project that I did at Western Washington University with two professors and 4 other students. I was in charge of finding the character models for the interviewers as well as creating realistic facial and body animations. I also programmed in a live feedback system that would tell the user in real time if they werenâ€™t making an appropriate amount of eye contact with the interviewers, talking too loud or too quiet, if their responses to a question was too long or too short and/or if there heart rate was spiking during certain time periods. These indicators all showed up in a picture frame inside the environment.

The goal of the project was to help individuals with autism spectrum disorder (ASD) prepare for job interviews. 90% of individuals with ASD are unemployed or underemployed. A big reason for this, is that most individuals with (ASD) struggle in job interviews. This was aimed to help them get interview practice from the comfort of their own home.

This project is an ongoing project at Western Washington University's NEAT lab. Because of this I cannot share the code for this project.
